## [RACE:ORRELLIE|NEAR_EXTINCT|UNDERGROUND] The Orrel'lie — Ancient Underground Civilization

**Pronunciation:** OR-el-lie.

**Overview:** Ancient race of underground-dwelling people. Sealed themselves in THE HOLDS across the world simultaneously — not fleeing a threat. The Holds were RESEARCH STATIONS. Distributed laboratories deliberately isolated so each population could study The Incumbent independently without cross-contaminating conclusions. No inter-Hold communication during sealed period by design. A predetermined opening date was calculated centuries in advance — all Holds open simultaneously, leaders convene the greatest scholarly summit in Orrel'lie history, compare independent findings. The mechanism worked perfectly. The date arrived on schedule. The bugs had gotten in. Almost nobody was left to walk out. The summit never happened. World event when Holds open: front page Whelk, both parties affected. Questions the world asks: Why simultaneously? Who built them? What's inside? Where are the builders?

**The Holds:** Desaturated teal stonework, root/vine overgrowth, dripping water, pools in crumbling rocky sinkholes, scattered Orrel'lie remains. Multi-level. Each contains: Orrel'lie puzzles (testing worthiness — do you understand our logic and language?), artifacts, lore, at least one Juvenile Queen. Puzzle path = safer. Brute force path = consequences. Hold locations across Calmaar and beyond TBD — Warren network map to be built.

**The Warrens:** Tunnel network connecting SOME Holds — not all. Infestation cut the project short. Tighter, more claustrophobic. Primary insect habitat. Incomplete passages end in collapse or emergency seals. Overland = slow and safe. Warren travel = fast and dangerous. Players won't know which Holds connect until already underground.

**The Incumbent Connection:** Orrel'lie found something existing beyond their gods, beyond their understanding of immortality — devoted their entire civilization to studying it. Believed proximity to something unkillable might yield the secret of unkillability. Core tragedy: The Incumbent never once acknowledged their existence. Generations of devotion to something that didn't register them. When their civilization collapsed they failed their sacred duty — maintaining the vigil. Survivors carry that failure alongside personal grief.

**Collapse:** Vel'urak infestation broke in unexpectedly. Not a cosmic force. Not a worthy opponent. Just hungry creatures exploiting a food source. Survivors spent generations staying alive, losing culture and history in the process. Deepest sections contain most intact records — also most heavily infested.

**Egression Zone Connection:** The central Hold — closest to The Incumbent, most advanced research site — sits beneath what became the Egression Zone crater. The Vossmere built their city directly on top of it, never knowing it was there. When the Vossmere shot down a Blubberhive, the corpse's decay chemicals seeped into the ground, mixed with Vossmere mutagenic drug compounds, corroded through to the Hold below — breaching it BEFORE the scheduled opening date. Fog spread upward. Vossmere transformed. Chemical corrosion continued eating downward over centuries, creating the crater. The Blubberhive corpse is still at the bottom, desiccated, surrounding The Incumbent.

**The Survivors:** When the Holds open, some Orrel'lie are still alive — not enough to repopulate, not enough to save the race as a people. They know their species is doomed. They emerge furious — not just grieving but ENRAGED. Their civilization paved over, their vigil failed, their race ending. Before they can even process the world they've emerged into, government scientists move in under "research and protection" framing. Lantash signs off on something that sounds reasonable on paper without knowing the full extent of what happens in those facilities. [DM] Scientists want Orrel'lie knowledge of The Incumbent specifically — methodology for existing near something outside causality. The most intact Hold (undiscovered, no Warren connection, surface entrance still sealed) contains the most complete records. Scientists desperately trying to locate it. [MALLEABLE]

**The Keche Relationship (DM only):** Contemporaries, not sequential — Keche on the surface, Orrel'lie underground, same era, same geography, parallel civilizations that never truly merged. The Keche were aware of the Orrel'lie and built defense structures directly over Hold entrances to monitor and suppress them. The Orrel'lie tried to warn the Keche about what they were building. Were dismissed — told to stay in their tunnels. Instead they went directly to Vaelmond. This is what finally gave him the complete picture and sent him running to the Scarred Crater with Aerresti. The Orrel'lie partially saved the world from something worse. Nobody knows. [DM] Some Keche defensive infrastructure built over Hold entrances is STILL ACTIVE — golems with thousand-year-old threat assessment protocols, built to keep Orrel'lie IN, not adventurers OUT. Initial response to surface dwellers is confused rather than immediately hostile. Some defenses are environmental rather than physical — disorientation fields, nausea, directional confusion — running on ambient broken network energy. Slowly weakening but not gone. Where both Keche and Orrel'lie stonework exist together, the architecture feels wrong in two distinct and contradictory ways. Scholars who find these locations think it's one civilization. It's two. Telling completely different stories in the same stone.

## [CREATURE:VELUURAK|BEAST|INFESTATION] Vel'urak (Amberbacks) — The Infestation

**Orrel'lie name:** Vel'urak — roughly "the consuming interruption" / "that which broke the vigil." Encodes both physical destruction AND failure of sacred purpose. Carved into warning stones throughout the Holds.
**Contemporary name:** Amberbacks — coined by a panicked journalist after the Holds open. Stuck immediately. Accurate, undignified, perfect.

**Appearance:** Large carnivorous burrowing insects. Dark red-orange coloring — high contrast against desaturated teal stonework. Exoskeleton wings and shells partially conceal amber-like substance on abdomen containing offspring in development. Amber glows dim orange while offspring develops — detaches when mature, carried to hatching hive room. Gravid parents visibly glow.

**Light weakness:** Torchlight insufficient. Requires magical light/radiant sources (Daylight spell, dawn magic, etc.) to drive back. Cannot breach surface — light sensitivity explains why they've never escaped despite centuries of opportunity. Every dungeon entrance becomes a natural threshold.

**Parent/offspring mechanic:** Damaging amber attached to living parent triggers ENRAGED FRENZY — parent abandons all self-preservation, commits entirely to destroying the threat. The tactically "clever" play backfires hard.

**Amber properties:** Millennium-old amber from creatures living adjacent to Incumbent energy. Unknown properties when exposed to sunlight for the first time. Valuable to alchemists, artificers (Val would want to BUILD with it), merchants (Copperbottom would pay a fortune). Mystery attached to every harvested piece.

**Seven Variants:**
1. **PUPA** — freshly hatched, fragile individually, catastrophic in numbers. No burrow, no flight. Pure frantic swarm.
2. **WORKER** — baseline adult. Warren maintenance and ambient threat. Burrow + run. No flight.
3. **SOLDIER** — armored, territorial, patrols the Holds. Crusher/grappler. Burrow + run. No flight — too heavy.
4. **AMBUSHER** — ceiling/wall clinger, drop predator. Light build, fast, long limbs. Flight/hover capable. Responsible for most unexplained Orrel'lie remains found in open corridors.
5. **GRAVID** — amber-carrying Worker variant. Dim orange glow visible. Enrages when offspring threatened. Amber destruction releases chemical alarm attracting every nearby insect.
6. **JUVENILE QUEEN** — mid-boss, one per Hold. Pre-reproductive — NO glow, NO amber, which means no warning. Fast, feral, unpredictable. Partial flight. Dangerous precisely because she hasn't grown into patience yet.
7. **HERALD** — rare, lean, non-combatant. Resonating abdomen produces subsonic pulse that travels through rock — alerts entire Hold to intruder presence. Flight capable. Kill it immediately or run.
8. **QUEEN** — true boss. Massive, partially immobile. Full bioluminescence across carapace from long proximity to Incumbent energy. Surrounded by Soldiers at all times. Possibly dim intelligence. No flight — too large. The hatching hive room is her throne.

## [HOMEBREW|HEALING_POTIONS]
- **Bonus Action** — consume quickly, heals rolled amount (standard potion: 2d4+2).
- **Full Action** — consume carefully, heals maximum amount (standard potion: 10 HP).
- Scales to potion tier — more powerful potions follow the same rule for their respective amounts.
- Flavor: taking it as a Bonus Action means you're in a hurry and don't utilize it fully.

## [HOMEBREW|ADVANTAGE_STACKING]
When Advantage comes from 2 or more sources, it stacks — but not as additional d20s. Every additional instance of Advantage past the first grants **+2 to the roll**.
- Example: 1st Advantage = roll 2d20 take highest. 2nd Advantage source = +2. 3rd = +2. 4th = +2. All stack.
- Rewards tactical coordination and setup without breaking bounded accuracy.

## [HOMEBREW|INSPIRATION_POINTS]
- Grants Advantage on any of your own rolls.
- No limit to how many you can hold at one time.
- Transferable to another party member at will — max 1 transfer per roll.

## [HOMEBREW|CONSUMABLE_BONUSES]
Same bonuses from consumables do NOT stack. Larger bonus overrides smaller bonus — does not add to it.
- Example: Two Sweetberry Pies (+1 Nature) = still only +1 Nature. A Super Sweetberry Pie (+2 Nature) overrides the +1 entirely.

## [HOMEBREW|REST_MECHANICS] Rest Activity System

Effects do NOT stack. Short Rest = choose ONE. Long Rest = choose TWO.

**SHORT REST OPTIONS:**
- **Rest** — Spend available Hit Dice to restore lost HP.
- **Weapon Improvement** — Sharpen sword, restring bow, etc. +1 damage OR +1 attack for up to 4 hours.
- **Armor Reinforcement** — Extra padding, tighten straps, etc. +1 AC for up to 4 hours.
- **Eat a Hearty Snack** — If supplies available, gain 1d4+1 Temporary HP until next Short or Long Rest.
- **Regain a Spell Slot, Point, or Dice** — Restore 1 Cantrip or 1st Level Spell Slot OR 1 Class-Specific Point/Dice (Sorcery Point, Superiority Die, etc.) OR regain 1 Hit Die for next Short Rest.
- **Meditate or Commune** — Reach out to favored deity or universal truth. Gain 1 Inspiration Point until next rest OR make a Religion roll for Insight/answers.

**LONG REST OPTIONS:**
- **Rest** — Restore all HP, restore ½ total Hit Dice, restore all Spell Slots/Class Points.
- **Weapon Improvement** — +1 damage OR +1 attack for up to 12 hours.
- **Armor Reinforcement** — +1 AC for up to 12 hours.
- **Gain an Additional Magic Item Charge** — Spend time attuning to go one charge past item's total. [DM] Use discretion on particularly powerful items — not all items qualify.
- **Do Some Research** — Roll Investigation to find information on a specific topic. DC set by DM based on topic obscurity and available resources.
- **Tend to an Injury** — Roll Medicine. Success clears a Minor Injury OR downgrades a Major Injury to Minor.
- **Prep a Skill** — Focus on a skill you anticipate needing. Gain temporary Proficiency in an unproficient skill until next Long Rest OR if already proficient, gain 1 Inspiration Point usable only with that skill.

**INJURY TIERS:**
- **Minor Injury** — Narrative impairment. Flavor and roleplay consequences. DM discretion on exact nature.
- **Major Injury** — Mechanical penalty. Triggered when a single hit deals ½ or more of max HP. Exact penalty DM discretion based on nature of injury.

## [CIVILIZATION:KECHE|EXTINCT|SURFACE|GLOBAL] The Keche — The Hidden Empire

**Pronunciation:** "Cache" — one syllable, rhymes with "cache." Scholars argue about the pronunciation constantly. The argument itself is load-bearing — nobody agrees because nobody alive has heard it spoken by its originators. [DM] The word and the Trece artifact name are linguistically connected — see ARTIFACT:TRECE. The civilization named itself after the act of following a path to a hidden destination. The network WAS the identity.

**Era:** Rose mid-late C.D., reached near-global reach in A.D. Contemporary with the Orrel'lie — not sequential. Keche on the surface, Orrel'lie underground, same era, same geography. Both civilizations were aware of each other and in conflict.

**Appearance/Character:** Conquerors, but primitive compared to Vossmere — magically inclined rather than technologically. Massive stonework. Mechanisms that still function with no visible power source. Geometry feels slightly wrong — not a construction flaw, an intentional optimization for magic flow rather than human use. Keche aesthetics prioritize function for a purpose nobody immediately understands. Structures appear cut off mid-build, set at impossible angles, half-disappeared — they didn't collapse, they're displaced.

**TRUE PURPOSE (DM ONLY):** Global conquest was never about territory or resources — it was about COVERAGE. The Keche were building a planetary magical siphon disguised as aqueducts and infrastructure. The empire wasn't an empire in the traditional sense — it was a PATH. The conquest was infrastructure. The destination was the Scarred Crater. Every node, every aqueduct, every "road" was designed to drain magical energy toward the crater center for a single catastrophic spell. Nobody outside the inner circle knew. Most Keche citizens thought they were building an empire.

**Collapse:** The Keche network's collapse triggered the Apotheosis. The distortion wave traveled outward along the network itself — damage proportional to connectivity. Major hub sites: catastrophically destroyed, geometry shifted partially out of phase with local reality. Minor nodes: mostly intact but slightly wrong. The network can never be restored — cost and structural impossibility both prohibitive. Occasional leakage from broken endpoints: unpredictable, unexplainable locally. Vaelmond recognizes it. Says nothing.

**Attunement:** No bloodline mechanic. Naturally magically inclined people find Keche mechanisms respond to them more readily — not destiny, not chosen, just conductivity. Like having perfect pitch. Aerresti lights up every Keche mechanism she gets near whether she wants to or not.

**Keche vs. Orrel'lie:** The Keche built defense structures directly over Orrel'lie Hold entrances to monitor and suppress them. The Orrel'lie tried to warn the Keche about what their network would do. Were dismissed. Warned Vaelmond directly instead. Some Keche suppression infrastructure over Hold entrances is still active — see RACE:ORRELLIE for full details.

**Vaelmond's Role:** Accepted Keche citizenship pragmatically — got close enough to investigate. Suspected something. The Orrel'lie gave him the final piece. He ran. Too late.

**What Scholars Know (In-World):** Ancient near-global civilization. Conquerors. Massive stonework. Mechanisms with no visible power source. Geometry slightly wrong. Wiped out/collapsed by something catastrophic (they call it "The Apotheosis" — they have no idea what it actually was). Left ruins worldwide. Active scholarly debate about pronunciation of their name. No connection made to the Apotheosis event being the network's doing — nobody has the full picture.

## [BESTIARY|DESIGN_RULES] Sacred Rules — Creature Design

**RULE 1 — NO MUNDANE CREATURES:**
Every creature in Caelos without exception carries a special quirk, ability, adaptation, or hybridization that makes it unique and interesting. "Regular" animals do not exist in this world. The world has been strange long before the players arrived. The mundane IS the extraordinary — there is no baseline boring in Caelos, only different scales of strange. This rule applies to ambient wildlife, combat encounters, companions, and cryptids equally.

**RULE 2 — CRYPTID SCARCITY IS LOAD-BEARING:**
Cryptids derive their terror from uncertainty and rarity. For every one cryptid encounter there should be many mundane (but still unique) creature encounters first. If cryptids appear constantly they become just another monster type — the dread evaporates. Maintain a rich, believable creature ecology first. Cryptids are the exception, never the rule. Their scarcity is what makes them mythic.

**RULE 3 — CRYPTID PRESENTATION PHILOSOPHY:**
Cryptids exist in the world as rumor, campfire story, children's cautionary tale, blurry photographs, and inconclusive eyewitness accounts from untrustworthy sources. No confirmed evidence. No authoritative statblocks in public knowledge. The truth is they're actually out there — but the world doesn't know that yet. When players encounter one, every story they've dismissed suddenly becomes a threat assessment.

**RULE 4 — REGIONAL CRYPTID ECOLOGY:**
Each region of Caelos has its own cryptid(s), reflecting regional folklore, geography, and history — similar to how each country in the real world has its own cryptid tradition. All cryptids are currently avoiding the Egression Zone due to the Oak Father and the Incumbent. Cryptid displacement (e.g. Drownout Heckler appearing in Greywood) is an involuntary map of Zone expansion — the world's early warning system that players can learn to read without ever being told that's what they're doing.

**RULE 5 — TOTAL CRYPTID COUNT:**
Not predetermined. World expands first. Count follows naturally.
